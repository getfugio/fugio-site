How we score accounts

We track the signals that indicate a company is ready to buy right now.

Each signal is weighted and combined into a single priority score. Your reps do not need to know how it works. They just need to know who to call first.

Growth signals

Hiring patterns

Active job postings for roles relevant to your buyer. Companies hiring for the titles that matter to your solution are companies in motion and ready to invest.

Growth signals

Recent funding

Closed a round in the last 90 days. Fresh capital means new budget, new pressure to hit numbers, and a leadership team ready to invest in the right things.

Growth signals

Headcount growth

Team size expanding faster than their peers. Growing companies often outpace their existing systems and need new solutions to keep up.

Technology signals

Tech stack changes

Tools added or dropped in the last 30 days. A company that just switched platforms or added new software is in motion and open to change.

Competitive signals

Competitor mentions

Actively referencing your competitors on their website or in job postings. These accounts already know the category. They are evaluating options right now.

Contact signals

Leadership changes

A new decision-maker in the last 90 days. New leaders evaluate vendors, reset priorities, and make decisions fast. One of the highest-intent windows in B2B.

Contact signals

LinkedIn activity

Key contacts posting about challenges, priorities, or initiatives relevant to what you sell. A warm signal that the conversation is already on their mind.

Intent signals

Job posting intelligence

Tools required, methodologies referenced, and pain points buried inside active job descriptions. A window into exactly what a company needs right now, in their own words.

Human connection signals

Personal common ground

Shared alma mater, mutual connections, and interests surfaced from LinkedIn and public profiles — hobbies, causes, communities, previous employers. The best outreach does not just reference what a company is doing. It acknowledges who the person is. We surface publicly available personal context so your reps can open a conversation that feels like it was written for that individual, not pasted from a template.
